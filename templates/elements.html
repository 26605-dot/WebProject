Elements

The piano is a beautifully versatile instrument that bridges the worlds of percussion and strings. When you press a key, it triggers a felt-covered hammer to strike high-tension steel strings inside, which then vibrate to produce sound. What makes the piano so special is its dynamic range—hence its original name, the pianoforte, which literally means "soft-loud." By changing how hard or soft you press the keys, you can evoke a massive spectrum of emotion. With 88 keys covering over seven octaves, it can play both deep, resonant bass lines and bright, shimmering melodies simultaneously. This incredible range makes it a foundational instrument for everything from classical masterpieces to modern pop and jazz.
